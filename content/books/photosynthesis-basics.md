# Photosynthesis Basics

Photosynthesis is a process used by plants and other organisms to convert light energy into chemical energy that, through cellular respiration, can later be released to fuel the organism's activities.

## How it works

1. **Light Absorption**: Chlorophyll in the leaves absorbs sunlight.
2. **Water Transport**: Roots soak up water from the soil.
3. **Carbon Dioxide**: The plant takes in carbon dioxide from the air.
4. **Glucose Production**: Using the energy from the sun, the plant turns water and carbon dioxide into glucose (sugar), which is its food.
5. **Oxygen Release**: As a byproduct, the plant releases oxygen back into the atmosphere.

## Why is it important?

Without photosynthesis, there would be little to no oxygen in the atmosphere. Most organisms depend on oxygen to survive.
